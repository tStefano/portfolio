THE HAGUE MUNICIPALITY SERVICE DESIGN

Context

The municipality of The Hague wants a team of 5 UX designers to redesign their city hall. Since its completion in 1992, new services and booths have been added to the space over time, without taking the overall layout into account.

Problem

Visitors have troubles with wayfinding in the atrium. They are not sure where they can find the services they need. In addition, the client doesn’t feel like the current setup makes the atrium welcoming, which they would like to achieve. They would also like to provide communication on a more personal level.

Solution

Four concepts based on relevant user needs: the Pinpoint, a new wayfinding machine that fits the use context and user goals through an interface with high learnability; the Seating Area is now ergonomical for the user and brings a welcoming feel; the Future Ground gives the opportunity to add user’s own opinion to the city and feeling immersed into an authentic experience; finally the Huts aim to achieve the “living room” feel of the atrium, as wanted by the client.

INTANGIBLE GIFT UNBOXING EXPERIENCE

Context

A Friend of Mine, Amsterdam-based agency, is cooperating with The Hague University students to get creative ideas for unboxing experiences. For this project the target group is one user only.

Problem

Find unique ways to provide a valuable experience when unboxing intangible gifts such as online subscriptions or event tickets.

Solution

An envelope including an origami sheet and some instructions. My user is passionate about origami and the crane is her favourite one, so she knows perfectly how to fold the sheet to build that. Once paper is folded and the crane is crafted, a URL will appear. The user is then able to go to that webpage and continue her experience digitally.

BUN.RUN DELIVERY SHARING PLATFORM

Context

Bun.Run is a Dutch logistics platform that connects Runners (private people who make themselves available to do any pickup/delivery with their own veichle) to end-users.

Problem

Bun.Run is missing a community where all its members can interact and communicate to each other in a useful and pleasant way.

Solution

A social media for deliveries where you can post an online-purchase delivery plan or join others’ plans and save some money on shipping costs. It uses location services because the closer a parcel will arrive, the better for the user.

DUTCH STORE WEBSITE REDESIGN

Context

has been a prominent supplier of Dutch products in the US for over 65 years, and with the progress of technology, it is now able to offer a complete line of typical food shipped directly to the customer's home.

Problem

The current website lacks of dedication to the experience during user's interaction, presenting usability problems and low efficiency.

Solution

A brand new design of their website with a human-centered way of thinking, that enhances the overall usability and provides valuable feelings to the user when getting in touch with their homeland through this digital experience.
